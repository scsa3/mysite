Description

M. Night Shyamalan brings together two of his standout original films—Unbreakable and Split— in this explosive comic book thriller. Elijah Price, also known as Mr. Glass (Samuel L. Jackson), finds David Dunn (Bruce Willis) pursuing Kevin Wendell Crumb's superhuman figure, The Beast (James McAvoy), in a series of escalating encounters. Price, armed with secrets critical to both men, emerges as a shadowy orchestrator.

Chef C Rock

Great Until The End

LOVED the majority of Glass but when the movie hits its climax it takes a disappointing turn that really undercuts the entire trilogy, let alone this film. Some intriguing questions are raised but you'll feel too soured by what you just saw to really care. *********SPOILER************** Everyone dies in the weakest way you could imagine (Bruce Willis drowns in a puddle in a parking lot...) apparently in a big let's-all-get-killed plan of Glass' who I guess knew about a shadowy organization that kills off supers before they can be seen by the world at large - even though we the audience learn of it in the last 8 minutes... Literally. So Glass basically does the Watchmen ending with footage of Glass, Crumb and David making it out into the world via David's son, Glass' mom and the girl The Beast spared in Split. So there you go. All the characters you care about are killed by villains you didn't know existed until the end and then a vague sequel is teased. 4 stars is too kind for this.
